Honours Dissertation



THE EFFECTIVENESS OF ONLINE ASSESSMENTS IN STUDENTS' SUCCESS DOING ONLINE PROGRAMMING COURSES IN HIGHER EDUCATION

Abstract
The study examines the effectiveness of online assessments in programming courses amidst the rising popularity of flexible and cost-effective learning methods. Utilizing qualitative research, it surveys 41 students and 4 lecturers to explore their experiences and perspectives. Thematic analysis reveals that current online assessment methods enhance student motivation, engagement, satisfaction, and academic achievement. However, there is room for improvement in assessing practical application of knowledge. Limitations include lecturer availability and assumptions about participant backgrounds. The findings offer valuable insights into optimizing online assessment design and implementation in programming education.
